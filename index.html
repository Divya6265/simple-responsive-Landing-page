Breakup projects into manageable pieces

Breaking up big projects into smaller, more manageable pieces helps you stay focused and productive. For example, if you've got a long assignment due, break it into a timeline of smaller assignments such as researching, outlining, writing, and proofreading.

Create a routine

Having an established routine helps you stay on track and be productive. A routine can include scheduling specific activities such as studying, cleaning your room, or going out for a walk. Set a designated time each day for completing each task and you'll be more productive!

Set clear goals and objectives

Before you start any project, take some time to set specific goals and objectives. It's easier to stay focused and productive when you have a clear roadmap to follow. Make sure you write down your goals and objectives so you can refer back to them as you progress.

Prioritize tasks

Having a lot of things on your plate can be overwhelming and lead to procrastination. Prioritize tasks according to their urgency and importance and tackle them one at a time so you don't get overwhelmed and lose focus.
